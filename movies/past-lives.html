Movie

Past Lives

In Yeon

2023 Celine Song Drama · Romance 106 min

Quietly devastating — Celine Song understands something about love that most films spend two hours getting wrong.

Past Lives is a film about the lives we do not live. About the versions of ourselves that exist only in the memory of the people who knew us before we became who we are. It is one of the most emotionally precise films I have seen in years, and it does everything quietly.

Celine Song's debut feature follows Nora and Hae Sung, childhood sweethearts in Seoul who are separated when Nora's family emigrates to Canada. The film moves across three stretches of time — childhood, early adulthood, and their mid-thirties — with a patience and confidence that most experienced directors never master.

What Worked

Greta Lee gives the performance of 2023. The final scene — which I will not describe — is among the most affecting endings I have witnessed in a cinema. It does not explain itself. It does not need to. The film's central concept of "in-yeon," a Korean notion of fate and connection across lifetimes, is introduced naturally and carries tremendous weight by the time the credits roll.

What Did Not

There is very little to criticize here. If anything, some viewers may find the middle section slightly slow. But that slowness is intentional — it mirrors the long, ambiguous distance of a relationship conducted over video calls across time zones.

Past Lives is the kind of film that stays with you for days. Watch it alone, or with someone you love. Either way, bring tissues.
