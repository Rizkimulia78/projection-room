Movie

Oppenheimer

2023 Christopher Nolan Drama · History · Thriller 180 min

A monumental achievement — Nolan constructs the tension of a ticking clock across three hours without once losing the audience.

There is a moment midway through Oppenheimer where the screen goes silent. Not quiet — silent. And in that silence, you feel the weight of what J. Robert Oppenheimer had built pressing down on the entire theatre. That is the kind of filmmaking Christopher Nolan is capable of at his best, and this is him at his best.

The film is structured across three interlocking timelines, and while that sounds like a recipe for confusion, Nolan manages it with the precision of a watchmaker. Cillian Murphy carries the weight of the entire film on his shoulders and does so without ever overplaying a single moment. His Oppenheimer is a man who understood the consequences of what he was doing and chose to proceed anyway — and Murphy makes you feel both the genius and the horror of that decision simultaneously.

What Worked

Ludwig Göransson's score is relentless and immersive. The Trinity test sequence is the best scene Nolan has ever directed. Robert Downey Jr. gives a career-best supporting performance as Lewis Strauss — calculating, wounded, and ultimately contemptible.

What Did Not

The film's female characters are underdeveloped. Emily Blunt and Florence Pugh are gifted actors given remarkably thin material. It is the one notable blind spot in an otherwise extraordinary film.

But these are minor complaints against something that functions as genuine cinema. Oppenheimer earns its three hours and then some.
