TV Show

The Bear

2022 Christopher Storer Drama · Comedy 3 Seasons Hulu / Disney+

Claustrophobic, relentless, and deeply human — the kitchen becomes a pressure cooker for every character's unresolved grief.

The Bear is not really a show about cooking. It is a show about people who cannot stop moving long enough to confront what is destroying them. The kitchen is just the arena — loud, hot, and unforgiving — that makes it impossible to hide.

Jeremy Allen White is extraordinary as Carmy, a fine-dining chef who inherits his brother's chaotic Chicago sandwich shop after a family tragedy. The first season is shot with a documentary urgency that makes you feel the heat and noise of a real kitchen. Every episode feels like a controlled explosion.

What Worked

The ensemble is exceptional across the board. Ayo Edebiri as Sydney is one of the best new characters in recent television — ambitious, wounded, and endlessly watchable. The Season 2 episode "Fishes," a single-location family Christmas dinner that somehow functions as both comedy and horror, is one of the finest single episodes of television in years.

What Did Not

Season 3 loses some of the raw energy of the first two. The pacing becomes uneven and certain character arcs feel stretched. The show is at its best when it is contained and urgent — when it tries to expand, it occasionally loses its footing.

But even at its most uneven, The Bear is better than most things on television. Watch it.
